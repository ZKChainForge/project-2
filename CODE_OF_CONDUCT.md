## Our Pledge

We as members, contributors, and leaders of the Zk-Arithmos community pledge to make participation in our community a **harassment-free experience for everyone**, regardless of:

- Age
- Body size
- Visible or invisible disability
- Ethnicity
- Sex characteristics
- Gender identity and expression
- Level of experience
- Education
- Socio-economic status
- Nationality
- Personal appearance
- Race
- Caste
- Color
- Religion
- Sexual identity and orientation
- Language
- Political beliefs
- Family status
- Veteran status
- Genetic information
- Neurodiversity

We pledge to act and interact in ways that contribute to an **open, welcoming, diverse, inclusive, and healthy community**.

We recognize that the tech industry, and cryptography in particular, has historically been unwelcoming to many groups. We are committed to actively working against this by fostering an environment where everyone can contribute, learn, and thrive.

### Our Values

**Respect**: We treat all community members with respect, regardless of their background or level of experience.

**Inclusion**: We actively work to include people from all backgrounds and make them feel welcome.

**Collaboration**: We believe in the power of working together and lifting each other up.

**Learning**: We encourage questions, support beginners, and share knowledge freely.

**Empathy**: We strive to understand others' perspectives and experiences.

**Accountability**: We take responsibility for our actions and their impact on others.

## Our Standards

### Positive Behaviors

Examples of behavior that contributes to a positive environment include:

#### Demonstrating Empathy and Kindness

- **Being welcoming**: Greet newcomers warmly and help them get oriented
- **Showing patience**: Remember that everyone was a beginner once
- **Offering help**: Proactively assist others who seem stuck or confused
- **Celebrating successes**: Acknowledge and celebrate community achievements
- **Expressing gratitude**: Thank contributors for their work and time

#### Being Respectful

- **Respecting differences**: Value diverse opinions, viewpoints, and experiences
- **Acknowledging contributions**: Give credit where credit is due
- **Listening actively**: Pay attention when others speak and seek to understand
- **Disagreeing constructively**: Focus on ideas, not individuals
- **Accepting decisions**: Accept community decisions gracefully, even when you disagree

#### Giving and Receiving Feedback

- **Being constructive**: Frame feedback in terms of improvement, not criticism
- **Being specific**: Provide actionable, concrete suggestions
- **Being kind**: Deliver feedback with empathy and respect
- **Being open**: Accept feedback graciously and consider it thoughtfully
- **Being timely**: Provide feedback when it's most useful

#### Taking Responsibility

- **Acknowledging mistakes**: Apologize sincerely when you make a mistake
- **Learning from errors**: Use mistakes as opportunities for growth
- **Making amends**: Take concrete steps to correct mistakes
- **Improving processes**: Suggest changes to prevent similar issues

#### Focusing on Community Good

- **Prioritizing community**: Consider what's best for the overall community
- **Sharing knowledge**: Contribute documentation, tutorials, and guidance
- **Mentoring others**: Help newer community members grow
- **Building bridges**: Connect people and ideas across the community

### Unacceptable Behaviors

Examples of unacceptable behavior include:

#### Harassment

- **Sexual harassment**: Unwelcome sexual attention, advances, or comments
- **Stalking**: Following someone online or offline in a threatening way
- **Doxxing**: Publishing private information without consent
- **Intimidation**: Making threats or engaging in bullying behavior
- **Unwanted contact**: Continuing to contact someone after being asked to stop

#### Discrimination

- **Slurs and epithets**: Using derogatory terms related to identity
- **Exclusionary jokes**: Making "jokes" at the expense of marginalized groups
- **Stereotyping**: Making assumptions based on someone's identity
- **Gatekeeping**: Excluding people based on their background or experience
- **Microaggressions**: Subtle discriminatory comments or behaviors

#### Disruptive Behavior

- **Trolling**: Posting inflammatory content to provoke reactions
- **Derailing**: Deliberately taking discussions off-topic
- **Spamming**: Posting excessive or irrelevant content
- **Brigading**: Organizing harassment campaigns
- **Sock puppeting**: Using multiple accounts to manipulate discussions

#### Dishonesty

- **Lying**: Providing false information intentionally
- **Plagiarism**: Taking credit for others' work
- **Misrepresentation**: Claiming credentials or experience you don't have
- **Bad faith arguments**: Engaging dishonestly in discussions

#### Violence and Threats

- **Threats of violence**: Threatening to harm someone physically
- **Encouraging self-harm**: Suggesting someone harm themselves
- **Glorifying violence**: Celebrating or promoting violence
- **Sharing violent content**: Posting disturbing or violent imagery

#### Privacy Violations

- **Sharing private communications**: Publishing DMs or emails without consent
- **Revealing personal information**: Sharing someone's real name, location, etc.
- **Recording without consent**: Recording calls or conversations secretly
- **Unauthorized access**: Accessing private spaces or information

### Professional Conduct

In addition to the standards above, we expect professional conduct in all community interactions:

#### Intellectual Discourse

- **Argue ideas, not people**: Focus on the merit of arguments, not personal attacks
- **Provide evidence**: Support claims with facts and references
- **Acknowledge uncertainty**: Be honest about what you don't know
- **Update beliefs**: Change your position when presented with compelling evidence
- **Avoid logical fallacies**: Engage in good-faith reasoning

#### Technical Discussions

- **Be patient with questions**: No question is too basic
- **Explain clearly**: Avoid unnecessary jargon; explain when you use it
- **Share context**: Provide background information to help understanding
- **Credit sources**: Acknowledge where ideas and code come from
- **Accept technical disagreements**: Reasonable people can disagree on technical matters

#### Code Review

- **Be constructive**: Focus on improvement, not criticism
- **Explain reasoning**: Help others understand why changes are suggested
- **Be respectful**: Remember there's a person behind every PR
- **Separate style from substance**: Distinguish between preferences and requirements
- **Assume good intent**: Assume contributors are trying their best

#### Setting Standards

- **Clarifying expectations**: Communicate standards clearly and consistently
- **Leading by example**: Model the behavior we expect from others

#### Enforcement

- **Consistent application**: Apply rules fairly and consistently
- **Timely response**: Respond to reports promptly
- **Proportional action**: Take appropriate action based on severity
- **Privacy protection**: Handle reports confidentially
- **Documentation**: Keep records of incidents and actions

#### Community Building

- **Fostering inclusion**: Actively work to make the community welcoming
- **Recognizing contributions**: Acknowledge and thank contributors
- **Resolving conflicts**: Help mediate disputes constructively
- **Supporting growth**: Help community members develop their skills
- **Seeking feedback**: Regularly ask for input on community health

### Community Responsibilities

All community members are responsible for:

#### Self-Regulation

- **Following guidelines**: Adhere to this Code of Conduct
- **Self-awareness**: Be mindful of how your behavior affects others
- **Self-improvement**: Learn from feedback and grow
- **Seeking help**: Ask for clarification when unsure about standards

#### Supporting Others

- **Welcoming newcomers**: Help new members feel included
- **Offering assistance**: Help others when you can
- **Providing feedback**: Give constructive feedback to help others improve
- **Reporting issues**: Report Code of Conduct violations when you see them

#### Contributing Positively

- **Adding value**: Contribute constructively to discussions
- **Staying on topic**: Keep discussions focused and relevant
- **Being patient**: Allow time for responses and understanding
- **Celebrating others**: Acknowledge and appreciate others' contributions

### Reporting Guidelines

If you experience or witness unacceptable behavior, please report it. **All reports will be taken seriously and handled confidentially.**

#### How to Report

**Primary Method: Email**

**Alternative Methods**

| Method | Use When | Contact |
|--------|----------|---------|
| **Discord DM** | Urgent situations | @ConductTeam |
| **GitHub** | For public incidents | @zk-arithmos/conduct-team |
| **Anonymous Form** | When you prefer anonymity | [form link] |
| **Individual Contact** | When you're uncomfortable with the team | Listed team members below |

#### When to Report

You should report:

- Any behavior that makes you feel unsafe, unwelcome, or uncomfortable
- Any behavior that violates this Code of Conduct
- Patterns of behavior that seem problematic
- Situations where you're unsure if something crosses a line

**You don't need to be the target to report.** If you witness behavior that affects others, please report it.

**When in doubt, report.** We'd rather receive a report that turns out to be a misunderstanding than miss a real problem.

### What to Include in a Report

To help us investigate effectively, please include:

#### Required Information

- **Your contact information** (unless anonymous)
- **Names/usernames** of people involved
- **What happened** - Description of the incident
- **When it happened** - Date(s) and time(s)
- **Where it happened** - Which platform/space

#### Helpful Additional Information

- **Evidence** - Screenshots, links, logs
- **Context** - Any relevant background
- **Witnesses** - Others who saw the incident
- **Pattern** - Whether this is part of ongoing behavior
- **Impact** - How this affected you or others
- **Desired outcome** - What you hope will happen

#### Example Report

```
From: reporter@example.com
To: conduct@zk-arithmos.dev
Subject: Code of Conduct Report

What happened:
During the community call on [date], [username] repeatedly interrupted 
and talked over [other username] when they were presenting. When 
[other username] asked them to stop, [username] made a dismissive 
comment about their technical ability.

When/Where:
Community Call, Discord #voice-call, January 15, 2024, around 4:15 PM UTC

Evidence:
The call was recorded. Relevant section is at timestamp 15:30-18:45.

Impact:
[Other username] seemed upset and left the call early. I felt 
uncomfortable witnessing this interaction.

Desired outcome:
I'd like the behavior to stop and for [other username] to receive 
an apology.
```

### Confidentiality

We take confidentiality seriously:

#### Our Commitments

- **Identity protection**: We will not reveal your identity without your consent
- **Need-to-know basis**: Only the Code of Conduct team will see reports
- **Secure handling**: Reports are stored securely and access-controlled
- **No retaliation**: We prohibit retaliation against reporters

#### Limitations

We may need to share information in certain circumstances:

- If required by law
- If there's an immediate safety threat
- If you give explicit permission
- In anonymized form for transparency reports

We will inform you if we need to make an exception and give you the opportunity to withdraw your report first.

### Investigation Process

When we receive a report, we follow this process:

#### Phase 1: Acknowledgment (24-48 hours)

1. Acknowledge receipt of the report
2. Assign a lead investigator
3. Assess if immediate action is needed
4. Provide estimated timeline

#### Phase 2: Investigation (1-2 weeks)

1. Review all provided evidence
2. Interview relevant parties (if appropriate)
3. Gather additional context
4. Consult with Code of Conduct team
5. Document findings

#### Phase 3: Decision (Within 1 week of completing investigation)

1. Determine if a violation occurred
2. Assess severity and context
3. Decide on appropriate action
4. Draft response communications

#### Phase 4: Action

1. Implement decided consequences
2. Notify the reporter of outcome
3. Notify the reported person of decision
4. Follow up as needed

#### Phase 5: Documentation

1. Record incident and outcome
2. Update any patterns
3. Review for process improvements
4. Include in transparency reports (anonymized)

### Enforcement Guidelines

Community leaders will follow these guidelines in determining consequences:

#### Factors Considered

| Factor | Consideration |
|--------|---------------|
| **Severity** | How serious was the violation? |
| **Intent** | Was it intentional or accidental? |
| **Pattern** | Is this a first offense or repeated behavior? |
| **Impact** | How were others affected? |
| **Vulnerability** | Were marginalized groups targeted? |
| **Acknowledgment** | Did the person acknowledge wrongdoing? |
| **Cooperation** | Did they cooperate with investigation? |
| **Remediation** | Have they taken steps to make amends? |

### Enforcement Actions

#### 1. Correction

**Trigger**: Minor, first-time violations; likely unintentional

**Actions**:
- Private written warning
- Explanation of violation
- Request for behavior change
- Guidance on appropriate behavior

**Example**: Unintentionally using non-inclusive language

#### 2. Warning

**Trigger**: More significant violation or repeated minor violations

**Actions**:
- Formal warning on record
- Required acknowledgment
- Specified behavior expectations
- Consequences for continued violations

**Example**: Repeated dismissive comments toward newcomers

#### 3. Temporary Restriction

**Trigger**: Serious violation or pattern of violations

**Actions**:
- Temporary ban from some or all community spaces
- Duration: Typically 1 week to 3 months
- Clear conditions for return
- Required commitment to behavior change

**Example**: Engaging in targeted harassment

#### 4. Temporary Ban

**Trigger**: Very serious violation or continued violations after warnings

**Actions**:
- Complete ban from all community spaces
- Duration: Typically 3-12 months
- Appeal process after minimum period
- Clear requirements for potential return

**Example**: Sustained harassment campaign

#### 5. Permanent Ban

**Trigger**: Severe violations, threats, or refusal to comply with previous actions

**Actions**:
- Permanent removal from all community spaces
- No appeal except for exceptional circumstances
- Documentation shared with relevant communities if appropriate

**Example**: Threats of violence, doxxing, illegal activity

### Appeals

If you disagree with an enforcement decision, you may appeal:

#### Appeal Process

1. **Submit appeal in writing** to conduct-appeals@zk-arithmos.dev
2. **Include reasoning**: Explain why you believe the decision was wrong
3. **Provide new evidence**: If available, include new information
4. **Wait for review**: Appeals are reviewed by uninvolved committee members
5. **Receive decision**: Final decisions are communicated in writing

#### Appeal Grounds

Valid grounds for appeal include:

- New evidence not available during initial investigation
- Procedural errors in the investigation
- Disproportionate consequences
- Incorrect interpretation of facts

#### Appeal Limitations

- Appeals must be submitted within 30 days of the decision
- Each person may submit one appeal per incident
- Appeal decisions are final
- Appeals do not stay enforcement actions during review

## Conflict Resolution

Not all conflicts are Code of Conduct violations. We encourage resolving disagreements constructively when possible.

### Informal Resolution

For minor interpersonal conflicts:

#### Step 1: Direct Communication

If safe and appropriate, try to resolve the issue directly:

1. **Choose the right moment**: Find a private, calm setting
2. **Use "I" statements**: Focus on how you feel, not blame
3. **Be specific**: Describe the behavior, not the person
4. **Listen actively**: Seek to understand their perspective
5. **Find common ground**: Look for solutions that work for both

**Example**: "I felt frustrated when my questions were dismissed in the meeting. Could we find a better way to discuss concerns?"

#### Step 2: Seek Peer Support

If direct communication doesn't work:

1. **Talk to a trusted community member**: Get perspective
2. **Consider involving a neutral third party**: Informal mediation
3. **Document the issue**: Keep notes of what's happening

#### Step 3: Escalate if Needed

If informal approaches don't work:

1. **Contact a maintainer**: They can provide guidance
2. **File a formal report**: If it's a Code of Conduct issue
3. **Disengage if necessary**: Your well-being comes first

### Formal Resolution

For more serious conflicts or when informal resolution fails:

#### Requesting Mediation

1. Contact conduct@zk-arithmos.dev
2. Explain the conflict and what you've tried
3. Request mediation services

#### Formal Complaint

If the conflict involves Code of Conduct violations:

1. File a formal report
2. Follow the investigation process
3. Participate as requested

### Mediation

For conflicts that aren't Code of Conduct violations but need help to resolve:

#### When Mediation Helps

- Personality conflicts affecting collaboration
- Disagreements about project direction
- Communication breakdowns
- Minor disputes that have escalated

#### Mediation Process

1. **Request**: Either party requests mediation
2. **Agreement**: Both parties agree to participate
3. **Mediator selection**: Neutral community member selected
4. **Session(s)**: Structured conversation(s) to resolve issues
5. **Agreement**: Parties agree on path forward
6. **Follow-up**: Check-in to ensure resolution holds

#### Mediation Principles

- **Voluntary**: Both parties must agree to participate
- **Confidential**: What's discussed stays private
- **Neutral**: Mediator doesn't take sides
- **Future-focused**: Goal is resolution, not blame
- **Good faith**: Both parties must engage honestly

## Community Guidelines

### Communication Guidelines

#### General Communication

| Do | Don't |
|-----|-------|
| Be clear and concise | Ramble or go off-topic |
| Use inclusive language | Use jargon without explanation |
| Assume good intent | Assume malice |
| Ask clarifying questions | Make assumptions |
| Stay on topic | Derail discussions |
| Be patient with delays | Demand immediate responses |

#### Written Communication

- **Be clear**: Write in complete, understandable sentences
- **Be concise**: Respect others' time
- **Be considerate**: Tone is hard to read; err on the side of kindness
- **Use formatting**: Break up long texts; use headers and bullets
- **Proofread**: Check for clarity before sending

#### Voice/Video Communication

- **Mute when not speaking**: Reduce background noise
- **Use video thoughtfully**: Video-on is encouraged but not required
- **Take turns**: Don't interrupt or talk over others
- **Be present**: Avoid multitasking during calls
- **Be patient with tech issues**: Connection problems happen

### Collaboration Guidelines

#### Working Together

- **Communicate early and often**: Share plans and progress
- **Respect others' time**: Be punctual and prepared
- **Follow through**: Do what you say you'll do
- **Ask for help**: It's okay not to know everything
- **Offer help**: Support others when you can

#### Code Collaboration

- **Follow contribution guidelines**: Read CONTRIBUTING.md
- **Respect code ownership**: Discuss major changes to others' code
- **Give credit**: Acknowledge others' contributions
- **Be open to feedback**: Code review is collaborative
- **Be constructive in reviews**: Focus on improvement

#### Decision Making

- **Seek consensus**: Try to find solutions everyone can support
- **Respect decisions**: Accept outcomes even when you disagree
- **Document decisions**: Record the what and why
- **Revisit when appropriate**: Decisions can be reconsidered with new information

### Feedback Guidelines

#### Giving Feedback

1. **Be timely**: Give feedback while it's still relevant
2. **Be specific**: Point to concrete examples
3. **Be constructive**: Focus on improvement
4. **Be kind**: Deliver feedback with empathy
5. **Be balanced**: Acknowledge positives too

**Constructive Feedback Formula**:
- **Observation**: "I noticed that..."
- **Impact**: "The effect was..."
- **Suggestion**: "What if we tried..."
- **Question**: "What do you think?"

#### Receiving Feedback

1. **Listen fully**: Don't interrupt or get defensive
2. **Seek understanding**: Ask clarifying questions
3. **Express appreciation**: Thank the person for their input
4. **Reflect**: Take time to consider the feedback
5. **Respond thoughtfully**: Decide what to act on

### Inclusive Language

Use language that welcomes everyone:

#### Gender-Inclusive Language

| Instead of | Use |
|------------|-----|
| Guys, gentlemen | Everyone, folks, team |
| He/she | They |
| Mankind | Humanity, people |
| Man-hours | Person-hours, work hours |
| Chairman | Chair, chairperson |

#### Ability-Inclusive Language

| Instead of | Use |
|------------|-----|
| Crazy, insane | Unexpected, surprising |
| Lame | Uncool, boring |
| Blind spot | Oversight, gap |
| Dummy/dumb | Example, placeholder |
| Sanity check | Quick check, validation |

#### Experience-Inclusive Language

| Instead of | Use |
|------------|-----|
| Obviously, clearly | Note that, be aware that |
| Simple, easy | Straightforward |
| Trivial | Small, minor |
| Just | (often unnecessary) |
| Any idiot could... | This can be done by... |

## Specific Space Guidelines

### GitHub

#### Issues and Discussions

- **Search first**: Check if your topic already exists
- **Use templates**: Fill out issue templates completely
- **Be descriptive**: Include all relevant information
- **Stay on topic**: Keep discussions focused
- **Be patient**: Maintainers are often volunteers

#### Virtual Events

- **Be present**: Avoid multitasking
- **Participate appropriately**: Follow the meeting format
- **Respect the speaker**: Save questions for appropriate times
- **Use chat appropriately**: Stay on topic, be kind

#### Speaking and Presenting

- **Represent well**: You represent the community
- **Be inclusive**: Make content accessible to all
- **Credit others**: Acknowledge sources and contributors
- **Handle questions respectfully**: Even challenging ones
- **Follow up**: Be available for questions afterward

### Social Media

#### Official Accounts

Content posted on official project accounts should:

- Be accurate and truthful
- Be inclusive and welcoming
- Represent community consensus
- Follow all applicable laws and platform rules

#### Personal Accounts

When discussing the project on personal accounts:

- **Be clear about your role**: Distinguish personal views from official positions
- **Be respectful**: Remember you're associated with the community
- **Don't harass**: Don't pile on or encourage others to
- **Protect privacy**: Don't share others' private information

## Supporting the Community

### Being an Ally

Being an ally means actively supporting marginalized community members:

#### What Allies Do

- **Listen**: Center the experiences of those affected
- **Learn**: Educate yourself about issues affecting others
- **Speak up**: Call out problematic behavior when you see it
- **Amplify**: Boost marginalized voices and contributions
- **Act**: Take concrete actions to support inclusion
- **Reflect**: Examine your own biases and behaviors

#### Allyship in Practice

| Situation | Ally Action |
|-----------|-------------|
| Someone is interrupted | "I'd like to hear [name] finish their point" |
| Credit is misattributed | "Actually, [name] came up with that idea" |
| Exclusionary joke made | "I don't think that's appropriate" |
| Newcomer seems lost | "Hi! Can I help you get started?" |
| Someone is talked over | "Let's make sure everyone has a chance to speak" |

#### Mistakes Happen

Everyone makes mistakes when trying to be supportive:

1. **Apologize sincerely**: "I'm sorry, I was wrong to say that"
2. **Learn from it**: Understand what you did wrong
3. **Do better**: Apply what you learned going forward
4. **Don't center yourself**: Don't expect comfort from those you hurt

### Bystander Intervention

When you witness unacceptable behavior:

#### The 5 D's of Bystander Intervention

**Distract**
- Change the subject
- Create a diversion
- Ask the target an unrelated question

**Delegate**
- Get help from someone else
- Alert a moderator or maintainer
- Ask others to support you

**Document**
- Screenshot the incident
- Note what happened, when, where
- Save evidence for reporting

**Delay**
- Check in with the target afterward
- Offer support and resources
- Help them report if they want to

**Direct**
- Address the behavior directly
- "That's not okay"
- "Please stop"

#### Choosing Your Approach

| Factor | Consider |
|--------|----------|
| Safety | Is direct intervention safe? |
| Relationship | Are you positioned to intervene effectively? |
| Severity | How urgent is the situation? |
| Target's wishes | What does the person being targeted want? |
| Your capacity | Do you have the energy and skills right now? |

### Mental Health Awareness

#### Supporting Mental Health

- **Be kind**: You never know what someone is going through
- **Reduce pressure**: Contributions are voluntary; respect limits
- **Encourage breaks**: Model healthy work-life balance
- **Destigmatize**: Normalize discussions about mental health
- **Share resources**: When appropriate, share helpful resources
